Minor Suit Transfer Bids

Transfers are used to show hands with a long minor. Super-accept (if willing to play 3NT opposite KQxxxx and out) by bidding what you like.

  • 2♠: transfer to clubs

    • 2NT: normal response

    • 3♣: preacceptance (good support)

  • 2NT: transfer to diamonds

    • 3♣: normal response (note: this is non-forcing, giving responder a way out of the auction with a 5-5 weak hand)

    • 3♦: preacceptance (good support)

After the transfer, responder can bid again to show extra length or strength:

  • New suit: shortness, mild slam try

  • 3NT: balanced, mild slam try

  • Raise to 4: invitational

  • Raise to 5: sign-off

When using this treatment, responder bids 2♣ (Stayman) with any game invitational hand. A rebid of 2NT over a 2♦ or 2♠ response shows an invitational hand that may or may not have a four-card major.
